# What is Agile?

The Agile is an iterative approach in software development which allows teams to deliver milestones as small phases. Instead of launching the product at the end of the software development life cycle, an Agile team delivers work in small but consumable increments. Once work begins, the Agile team cycles through process of planning, executing, and evaluating. In Agile continuous collaboration between the team and stakeholders is vital.

# Agile methodologies overview

In Agile Manifesto consists of 4 values and 12 principles that guide the Agile Philosophy.

# 4 Values of Agile

  • Individuals and interactions over process and tools.
  • Working software over comprehensive documentation.
  • Customer collaboration over contrast negotiation.
  • Responding to change over following a plan. By responding to customer needs and adapting to change more efficiently, these vales help drive a development process that reliably delivers quality products and happy customers. In the Capstone context, the board may request your team to change your plan to meet their vision of the product, it is therefore crucial to relay on the 4 values of Agile by responding to change, collaborating with the board by asking questions and arranging a meeting when required to clearly define their requirements.

# 12 Principles of Agile.

  • An Agile team should follow these principles
  • First priority of an Agile team is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.
  • Welcome and harness changes for the customers competitive advantage, even late in development. Deliver working software frequently, from a couple of weeks to a couple of months, with preference to the shorter timescale. In Capstone may differ and might not mean a working software depending where your team started.
  • The business people and developers need to work collaboratively daily throughout the project.
  • Build projects around motivated individuals. Give them the environment and support they need, and trust them to get the job done.
  • The most efficient and effective method of conveying information to and within a development team is the face-to-face conversation.
  • Working software is the primary measure of progress.
  • Agile processes promote sustainable development. The sponsors, developers, and users should be able to maintain a constant pace indefinitely.
  • Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design enhances agility.
  • Simplicity—the art of maximizing the amount of work not done—is essential.
  • The best architectures, requirements, and designs merge from self-organizing teams.
  • At regular intervals, the team reflects on how to become more effective, then tunes and adjusts its behavior accordingly.
