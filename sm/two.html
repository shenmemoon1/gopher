# Scrum Master Training

TIP

# What is Scrum Framework?

Scrum is a framework that helps teams work together. Scrum encourages teams to learn through experiences, self-organise while working on a problem, and reflect on their wins and losses to continuously improve. Scrum describes a set of meetings, tools, and roles that work in concert to help teams structure and manage their work. Product owner prioritized a backlog of work for the team to do. Every two weeks or so the team looks at the backlog and decides what they can accomplish in the next two weeks. The team develops and tests solutions to the backlog items until done and ready for use. At the end of the two weeks the team demonstrates its completed work to the product owner and stakeholders. Finally, the team reflect on how things went during the two weeks and how it can improve.

# Scrum Roles

Scrum roles describe the key responsibilities of the team members. They are not be confused with job titles. The three roles provide a minimum definition of responsibilities and accountability to allow the team to effectively deliver work. The three roles are Scrum Master, Product Owner, and Developer. These members can still have many different job titles in the company. For example, the Scrum Master might help with development, and the Developers might help with leadership.

# Scrum Master

Protects the team and the process The facilitator and keeps the team within the scrum scope Keeps the team working at a sustainable pace Acts as a spokesperson for the team. First escalation point for the team. Helps remove roadblocks Focuses on how the team does the work The scrum master holds the team accountable. Organizes and facilitates the scrum events.

# Product Owner

Act as full-time business representative Reviews the team’s work and either accepts or asks for it to be fixed Ensures highest value is delivered Interacting with stakeholders on daily basis. Maintains the product vision Defines and manages the backlog of items to be done and the prioritizing the tasks.

# Developers

The Development Team is made up by professionals who work to deliver a potentially releasable increment of “Done” products at the end of each Sprint. It does not exclusively mean software developers. Achieve the goals defined for each sprint Attend daily scrum meetings reporting the planned tasks for each day Assure to Product Owner and Scrum Master that the allocated work is being performed as planned Provide inputs on the creation of User Stories to the Product Owner and understand the User Stories Estimate User Stories approved by the Product Owner Commit User Stories to be done in a sprint and develop the list of tasks based on the User Story Report to the Scrum Master impediments to the progress of the project, if any Agree on the length of sprint with the other Scrum Team members  Develop the Sprint Backlog and the Sprint Burndown Chart Create the deliverables Update Burndown Chart, Scrum Board, and Impediment Log Submit Change Requests, if any Participate in Prioritized Product Backlog Review Meetings Participate in the Retrospect Project Meeting

# Sprint Events

Scrum prescribes 5 events - the Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review and Sprint Retrospective.

# Sprint

The time box event where at the end of it, you will be accomplishing your goals and produce an inspectable increment of value. We recommend 2 weeks per Sprint.

TIP

# Sprint Planning

The planning that takes place to determine what will be in your Sprint backlog. We recommend at least an hour. The work and how to accomplish it goes into the Sprint backlog. It is assigned to an owner. Sprint planning is an event in scrum that kicks off the sprint. The purpose of sprint planning is to define what can be delivered in the sprint and how that work will be achieved. Sprint planning is done in collaboration with the whole scrum team. Set realistic goals by defining;

# The What

–  The product owner describes the objective(or goal) of the sprint and what backlog items contribute to that goal. The scrum team decides what can be done in the coming sprint and what they will do during the sprint to make that happen.

# The How

– The development team plans the work necessary to deliver the sprint goal. Ultimately, the resulting sprint plan is a negotiation between the development team and product owner based on value and effort.

# The Who

– Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development team must attend. The product owner defines the goal based on the value that they seek. The development team needs to understand how they can or cannot deliver that goal. If either is missing from this event it makes planning the sprint almost impossible.

# The Inputs

– A great starting point for the sprint plan is the product backlog as it provides a list of ‘stuff’ that could potentially be part of the current sprint. The team should also look at the existing work done in the increment and have a view to capacity.

# The Outputs

– The most important outcome for the sprint planning meeting is that the team can describe the goal of the sprint and how it will start working toward that goal. The team agrees on an acceptance criteria for each task.

TIP

# Daily Scrum

A 15 minutes event to come together as a team to figure out the progress of the team towards the goal and to identify any blockers. This Daily Scrum is used for re-planning the plan. Discussions on the resolution of the blocker is followed-up after this event.

# Everyone including the Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Developers must attend this meeting.

The Scrum Master facilitates the daily meeting. Provide a clear breakdown of the work the scrum team has carried out Track how fast the team as a whole and individual members are working Clear out obstacles to the work that’ll be carried out that day Schedule tasks to be carried out quickly The  Scrum Master asks each team member answer these questions

  • What I worked on yesterday/today.
  • What I plan to work on Tomorrow
  • Any blockers.

TIP

# Sprint Review

This is where you get to demo your work to the stakeholders, ELT and board of directors to get feedback for your work so that you can improve on it in the next Sprint. Sprint Retrospective This is the most important event for reflecting on the work which your team has done and how you have gone about doing it. The purpose is to make improvements to the way you work so that you can accelerate as a team and make the experience more pleasant in the following Sprint.

  • Attendees include the Scrum Team and key stakeholders invited by the Product Owner;
  • Members of the Scrum Team explain what Product Backlog items have been “Done” and what has not been “Done”;
  • The developers discuss what went well during the Sprint, what problems it ran into, and how those problems were solved;
  • The Developers demonstrate the work that it has “Done” and answers questions about the Increment;
  • The entire group collaborates on what to do next, so that the Sprint Review provides valuable input to subsequent sprint planning;

TIP

# How to facilitate the Sprint Retrospective

Sprint retrospective takes place on the last day of the sprint. It is an opportunity for reflection and check what went well and what didn’t go well. These questions are asked during the sprint retrospective.

  • What work has been done well in this sprint?
  • What work hasn’t been done well?
  • What should we start doing to improve?

Ensure the suggested improvements are implemented in the next sprint.

# Tips and Tricks- Teams Avalibity.

Collect your teams availability, use whatever method works for you, I like to use "https://www.when2meet.com/" as I can visually see how many people are available at a specific time. Unfortunately not everyone will be available on the same time. Select the time majority are available about 80-90% of your team.

# Tips and Tricks- Scheduling Meetings.

TIP

I recommend making an announcement on the Teams channel 2-3 days prior to the sprint planning date, which highlights the times and days of each sprint event.

TIP

Use the calendar to invite team members to each sprint event. Ask them all to accept the calendar invite so they can get a reminder. In the calendar invite state the meeting agenda.

# Tips and Tricks – team mates not contributing

  • Keep a record of those not attending meetings without notice.
  • Keep an eye on those who speak the most and find ways to allow everyone to speak during meetings. Some tricks include asking each person's opinion.
  • Keep in mind that people are shy, or English is their second, third, or fourth language.  Contribution is not only about talking during meetings.
  • Not speaking in a meeting does not always mean they are not valuable and will not contribute.
  • Find out the strengths of the quiet team members.
  • Allocate a 'card lead' responsible for overseeing the completion of that task.
  • You can report members for not contributing and unprofessional behaviour.

# Tips and Tricks – Facilitating meetings

  • Keep to the agenda and prescribed time. Otherwise, daily stand ups will go for more than 15-20 minutes.
  • Establish a meeting etiquette/agreement at the beginning of the trimester.
  • Allocate minutes taking to another person - so you are fully engaged in facilitating the meeting.
  • Leave questions to the end.
  • Don't dismiss people's ideas.

# Tips and Tricks – Progress Report

If anyone cannot make the daily-stand up. Create a progress report thread, ask them to provide their update there.

# Tips and Tricks – Trello Board

  • Product Owner needs to get the backlog ready.
  • Label them Sprint 1, 2, etc.
  • Label them from low-high priority
